How TPA Partnerships Work

A more structured workflow for claim-driven restoration projects.

TPA partnerships are designed to make restoration projects more efficient by aligning the homeowner, carrier, adjuster, and restoration contractor around a clearer process. In practical terms, the TPA assigns the claim, the contractor contacts the homeowner, documentation is collected and shared, the work is completed, and the project is reviewed before closeout.

This kind of structure helps support timely communication, stronger documentation, and better accountability from first assignment through final review.

How the process works

  • Claim assignment and dispatch
  • Prompt homeowner contact
  • Documentation and communication
  • Repair and rebuild
  • Quality review and closeout
Why TPA Programs Matter

More consistency, clearer reporting, and a better claim experience.

For many restoration projects, TPA involvement creates a more predictable workflow. It helps make sure communication stays active, documentation is shared properly, and quality expectations are met throughout the life of the project.

That added structure can be especially valuable on emergency jobs where timing, reporting, and next-step coordination all matter at once.

Benefits

  • Clearer communication between stakeholders
  • Better documentation and reporting
  • More organized project flow
